Resource categories

Path Forward provides information about community resources that can help individuals experiencing homelessness access essential services and support.

Explore the categories below to learn about different types of assistance that may be available in your area.

Access to safe and stable housing is often the first step toward long-term stability. Many communities offer emergency shelters, transitional housing programs, and housing assistance services designed to help individuals and families facing housing insecurity.

Examples of available resources may include:

  • Emergency shelters
  • Transitional housing programs
  • Family housing services
  • Housing assistance organizations
  • Community outreach programs

If you are experiencing a housing crisis, contact local shelters and support organizations to learn about available options and eligibility requirements.

Food assistance programs help individuals and families meet their nutritional needs during difficult times. Local organizations and community programs often provide meals, groceries, and other forms of support.

Examples of available resources may include:

  • Food banks
  • Community meal programs
  • Soup kitchens
  • Grocery assistance programs
  • Emergency food distribution services

These resources can help ensure that individuals and families have access to nutritious food while working toward greater stability.

Employment services can help individuals develop skills, find job opportunities, and work toward financial independence. Many organizations provide support throughout the job search process.

Examples of available resources may include:

  • Job search assistance
  • Resume and interview preparation
  • Career counseling
  • Workforce development programs
  • Skills training and certification programs

Mental health support is an important part of overall well-being. Individuals experiencing homelessness may face unique challenges that can affect emotional and mental health.

Examples of available resources may include:

  • Counseling services
  • Mental health clinics
  • Support groups
  • Crisis intervention programs
  • Substance use recovery services

Seeking support is an important step toward improving mental wellness and building long-term stability. If immediate assistance is needed, contact local emergency services or crisis support organizations.

If you are unsure which resource category best fits your situation, explore all available options or reach out through our Contact page for additional information and guidance.
